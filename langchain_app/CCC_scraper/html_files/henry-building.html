Henry Building

309 SW 4th Ave.
Portland, OR 97204

 

The Fair Market waitlist for the Henry will open on Thursday 1/11/2024 at 8:00 am PDT.

 

The historic Henry Building is a six-story building in downtown Portland, conveniently located near multiple bus and MAX lines and in close proximity to services. The Henry reopened in 2020 after undergoing a seismic rehab. All units now feature new fixtures and appliances. All units are single-room occupancy with shared bathroom facilities.

Community Overview

  • Number of units: 173
  • Built: 1909 (rehab in 2020)
  • Public transportation: Centrally located near multiple bus routes and MAX stations (0.4 mile)
  • Grocery stores: Safeway (0.8 mile), Fred Meyer (0.9 mile), Target Grocery (0.5 mile), Whole Foods (0.5 mile)
  • Parks: South Park Blocks (0.7 mile), Tom McCall Waterfront Park (0.4 mile), Washington Park (1.8 miles)
  • Health facilities: Legacy Good Samaritan (1.6 miles), Providence Medical Center, Old Town Clinic (0.3 miles), Old Town Recovery Center (0.3 miles)
  • Other: Portland State University Farmers Market (1.0 mile)

In-person. Bring your completed and signed pre-application materials to the CCC Housing Office at 523 NW Everett St., Portland OR, 97209.

Note: CCC strongly encourages applicants to submit pre-application forms by email so there is a electronic record and we can provide a receipt. CCC will not accept faxed applications or those submitted directly at the Henry.

Amenities

  • Parking
  • Laundry
  • Community room
  • Smoking/non-smoking
  • Wheelchair accessible
  • On-site services
  • On-site staff
  • Pets are not allowed at the Henry Building.  Applicants/Tenants may request service or assistance animals as a reasonable accommodation when necessary to support a verified disability. All requests are subject to management approval.

Eligibility and screening

  • A security deposit is not required at the Henry Building.
  • For more information on screening requirements, refer to the Henry Building Criteria.

Units at the Henry Building are income and rent restricted based on a percentage of Median Family Income (%MFI) for Multnomah County. Households must earn annual income below the corresponding %MFI to be eligible for the unit.
