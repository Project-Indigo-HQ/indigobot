Chez Ami Apartments

8358 SE Causey Ave.
Happy Valley, OR 97086

 

All Chez Ami referrals are managed by the Clackamas County Coordinated Housing Access. Referrals (for both fair market and PBV’s) go through the Clackamas County Coordinated Housing Access, information on applying for referrals can be found here.

Chez Ami is a two story alcohol- and drug-free community located in Happy Valley, Oregon. All of the studio units at Chez Ami are rent subsidized with tenants paying rent based on their income, offering services through Clackamas County Behavioral Health.

Community Overview

  • Number of units: 40
  • Year built: 2001        
  • Public transportation: 33, 71 and 72 bus lines
  • Grocery stores: Natural Grocers (0.1 mile), WinCo Foods (0.2 mile), Fred Meyer (1.3 mile), Walmart (0.8 mile)
  • Parks: Happy Valley Park (4.0 miles), Mount Talbert Nature Park (2.5 miles)
  • Health facilities: Kaiser Sunnyside Medical Center (1.2 miles)

Waitlists

The waitlist for Section 8 units is managed by Clackamas County Coordinated Housing Access (CHA).

Filling out the form at the link below helps get the information we need to help you. It is not mandatory. If you prefer to talk to someone, you can also call 503-655-8575 and leave a message.

For questions about the process, you can email cha@clackamas.us

Fill out the online at www.clackamas.us.

Amenities

  • Parking
  • Laundry
  • Community room
  • Smoking/non-smoking
  • Wheelchair accessible
  • On-site services
  • On-site staff

Pets are not allowed at Chez Ami.  Applicants/Tenants may request service or assistance animals as a reasonable accommodation when necessary to support a verified disability. All requests are subject to management approval.

Eligibility and screening

  • There is a $125 security deposit at Chez Ami.
  • The building is an alcohol- and drug-free community.
  • For more information on screening requirements, refer to the Chez Ami Building Criteria/Tenant Selection Plan.

Units at the Chez Ami are income and rent restricted based on a percentage of Median Family Income (%MFI) for Clackamas County. Households must earn annual income below the corresponding %MFI to be eligible for the unit. Click here for information to help you determine the maximum income limit.
