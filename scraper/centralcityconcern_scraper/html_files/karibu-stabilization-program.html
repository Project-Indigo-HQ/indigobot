Karibu Stabilization Program

Location
2020 SE Powell Blvd.
Portland, OR 97202

Contact
(503) 546-9975
Front desk staffed 24 hours a day to answer any calls!

Submit a Referral

The Karibu Stabilization for Treatment Preparation (STP) program offers transitional, dorm-style housing with 15 beds. This program is designed to provide individualized, culturally specific services for Black/African American men (ages 18+) who have behavioral health needs and are involved in, or at risk of involvement with, the criminal justice system. Our mission is to provide high-quality services that are inclusive and culturally appropriate, helping our community achieve better health. 

We recognize the ongoing stigma and discrimination faced by Black/African Americans, especially those involved with the justice system. These challenges, coupled with a lack of culturally affirming care, lead to significant health disparities. During their stay, for up to 9 months, participants engage in programming, while working on securing next-step housing.

Program Information

Services Eligibility Screening Submit a Referral

Services

  • 24/7 supportive housing in Karibu facility  
  • Mental health and substance use disorder support  
  • Housing, employment and clinical case management 
  • Culturally specific treatment and stabilization activities  
  • Family and community connection  
  • Individualized peer services  
  • Group therapy and skill development 
  • Service navigation and advocacy 
  • Crisis planning and intervention 

Eligibility

To join the Karibu program, individuals must meet the following criteria: 

  • Adult Black/African American male (18 years or older) 
  • At risk of, or currently involved with, the criminal justice system 
  • Experiencing unstable housing 
  • Identified mental health and/or substance use disorder needs  
    • Excluding behaviors, symptoms, and treatment needs qualifying for “Level D” as defined by Health Share of Oregon 

Screening 

In addition to the eligibility criteria, we consider the following factors: 

Safety 

  • History of aggressive behaviors 
  • Ongoing sexual or predatory behaviors 
  • Ability to navigate a dorm-style, shared living space 

 Client Readiness 

  • Willingness to participate in up to 30 hours a week of programming, including individual/group counseling, Karibu community groups, support groups, outings, etc. 
  • Commitment to engage in treatment for substance use disorder (SUD) and/or mental health (MH) needs 

Self-Sufficiency 

  • Ability to manage Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) 
  • Capability to take medication independently
