Old Town Clinic: Health Care

Hours
Monday, Tuesday, Thursday and Friday, 8 am to 5 pm
Wednesday, 9 am to 5 pm
Closed holidays

Location
727 W Burnside St.
Portland, OR 97209

Contact
(503) 228-4533

Located in the heart of the Old Town Chinatown neighborhood, Old Town Clinic can meet most patients’ health care needs in a single location. Our providers offer physical health care, mental health and addiction treatment, and many other services. We provide judgement-free treatment and support on a sliding-fee scale.

Program Information

Services Pharmacy How to Access Care Insurance Information About Old Town Clinic

Services

  • Primary care/routine health screenings
  • Managing chronic health conditions
  • Treatment for injuries
  • Minor medical procedures
  • Mental health counseling and medication or referral to a specialist if you need more support
  • Addiction/substance use treatment, including medication-supported recovery with Suboxone (buprenorphine)
  • Consultation with a pharmacist about your prescriptions
  • On-site medication refills
  • Hepatitis C treatment
  • Acupuncture
  • Consumer drug take-back (open to CCC patients as well as the general public)

Pharmacy

(503) 944-4465

Monday, Tuesday, Thursday and Friday, 8:30 am to 5:30 pm
Wednesday, 9:30 am to 5:30 pm

Our on-site pharmacy makes it easy to get your CCC prescriptions filled. Our knowledgeable pharmacists can answer any questions you have about your medications.

How to Access Care

We offer in-person, phone and video appointments during our regular clinic hours. Call (503) 228-4533 to schedule a visit.

Insurance Information

We don’t turn anyone away for lack of insurance or inability to pay. We accept Oregon Health Plan, Medicare, self-pay and some private insurance. Need coverage? We can help you get set up.

About Old Town Clinic

Old Town Clinic offers health care with heart, centrally located in the heart of the Old Town Chinatown neighborhood.

Ralph’s story

Not that long ago, Ralph was running out of chances after failing to kick his meth habit and stop drinking. Today, he’s sober and has a good job making COVID-19 face shields for hospital workers — all thanks to the long-term support he found through CCC’s recovery, housing, health and employment services.
